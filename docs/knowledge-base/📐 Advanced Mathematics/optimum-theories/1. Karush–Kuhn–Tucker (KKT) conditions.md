## 1. Concept of KKT  Condition
### (1) problems for solve 
The standard-form  of  optimization problem is given as [^1] :
$$\Large
\boxed{\begin{align}
\text{minimize}  &  \space f(x), \\
s.t.  &  \quad g_{i}(x) \leq  0  \\
& \quad   h_{j}(x)  =0
\end{align}} \tag{1.1.1}
$$
Where $f(x)$ is objective, $g$ are inequality constraints. 

Then the **Karush-Kuhn-Tucker Condition**[^1], Firstly, we can construct the following Lagrangian function : 
$$
\mathcal{L}(x, \mu ,\lambda) = f(x) + \mu^{T} g(x) + \lambda^{T} h(x) = \mathcal{L}(x, \alpha) \tag{1.1.2}
$$
Re-write <span class="equation-citator-citation equation-citator-cite-equation" data-ec-kind="eq" data-ec-refs='[{&quot;file&quot;:null,&quot;crossFile&quot;:null,&quot;tag&quot;:&quot;1.1.2&quot;}]' style="cursor: default; color: #4199df;">(<span style="text-decoration: none; cursor: pointer; color: #4199df;">1.1.2</span>)</span> gives : 
$$
\mathcal{L}  = \left[\begin{matrix}
\mu ^{T}  &  \lambda^{T}
\end{matrix}\right] \left[\begin{matrix}
g(x)  \\
h(x)
\end{matrix}\right] = \alpha^{T} \left[\begin{matrix}
g(x)  \\
h(x)
\end{matrix}\right] \tag{1.1.3}
$$

### (2) The KKT condition 
The <b><mark style='background: transparent; color: red'>Karush-Kuhn-Tucker Condition</mark></b> state the following sufficient and  necessary conditions : 

1. Sufficiency : If $(x^{*}, \alpha^{*})$ is **the saddle point of** $L (x, \alpha)$  in $x \in  X, \mu \geq  0$ then $x^{*}$ is an optimal vector for the optimization problem <span class="equation-citator-citation equation-citator-cite-equation" data-ec-kind="eq" data-ec-refs='[{&quot;file&quot;:null,&quot;crossFile&quot;:null,&quot;tag&quot;:&quot;1.1.1&quot;}]' style="cursor: default; color: #4199df;">(<span style="text-decoration: none; cursor: pointer; color: #4199df;">1.1.1</span>)</span>
2. Necessity : Suppose $f(x)$  and $g_{i}(x)$,  are **convex** in $X$ , and there exists :
$$x_{0} \in \text{relint} (X) \qquad  \text{s.t.}\quad   g (x_{0}) < 0 \tag{1.2.1} $$
that is, slater's condition[^4] holds, Then with an optimal vector $x^{*}$,  for the optimization problem, there is a vector  
$$
\alpha^{*} = \left[\begin{matrix}
\mu^{*}  \\
\lambda^{*}
\end{matrix}\right] \tag{1.2.2}
$$
satisfying $\mu^{*} \geq  0$,  and  the  $(x^{*}), \alpha$ **is the saddle point** of $\mathcal{L}(x, \alpha)$ 

### (3) Necessary condition  
For the part :
$$\min_{\alpha, \beta \geq  0}  L(x, \mu, \lambda) =  f(x) + \sum^{m}_{i=1} \mu_{i}g_{i}(x) + \sum^{k}_{j=1} \lambda_{j} h_{j}(x)\qquad (g_{i} \leq   0, h_{j} = 0) \tag{1.3.1} $$
then : 
$$\boxed{0 \in  \partial \mathcal{L}(x^{*}) =  \partial f(x^{*}) + \sum_{j = 1}^{l} \lambda_{j} \partial h_{j}(x^{*}) + \sum_{i = 1}^{m} \mu_{i} \partial g_{i} (x^{*}) = f(x) \tag{1.3.2}}$$
<b><mark style='background: transparent; color: orange'>For  minimizing</mark></b>  $f(x)$,  we have following conditions : 
$$\boxed{\Large
\begin{cases}
h_{j} (x^{*}) = 0 \qquad g_{i} (x^{*}) \leq  0   &  \quad  \text{primary feasibility} \\
\mu_{i} (x^{*})  \geq  0   & \qquad  \text{dual feasibility} \\
\sum_{i = 1}^{m} \mu_{i}^{*} g_{i}(x^{*}) = 0 \qquad &  \text{Complementary Slackness}
\end{cases} \tag{1.3.3}}
$$
The equation <span class="equation-citator-citation equation-citator-cite-equation" data-ec-kind="eq" data-ec-refs='[{&quot;file&quot;:null,&quot;crossFile&quot;:null,&quot;tag&quot;:&quot;1.3.2&quot;},{&quot;file&quot;:null,&quot;crossFile&quot;:null,&quot;tag&quot;:&quot;1.3.3&quot;}]' style="cursor: default; color: #4199df;">(<span style="text-decoration: none; cursor: pointer; color: #4199df;">1.3.2~3</span>)</span>, or often <span class="equation-citator-citation equation-citator-cite-equation" data-ec-kind="eq" data-ec-refs='[{&quot;file&quot;:null,&quot;crossFile&quot;:null,&quot;tag&quot;:&quot;1.3.3&quot;}]' style="cursor: default; color: #4199df;">(<span style="text-decoration: none; cursor: pointer; color: #4199df;">1.3.3</span>)</span> <b><mark style='background: transparent; color: red'>are called KKT condition </mark></b>

> [!NOTE] Complementary  Conditions 
> We note For (1.3.3), the **KKT conditions for inequality boundaries are complementary conditions** : 
> $$\xi_{j} \geq 0 \qquad  x_{j} - \alpha_{j} \geq  0 \qquad  \xi_{j} (x_{j} - \alpha_{j}) = 0$$
> For an illustration, see [^3]

**Feasibility** : 
- This is a <b><mark style='background: transparent; color: orange'>necessary condition for finding the optimal solution</mark></b>, but it only holds true **if the problem satisfies certain regularity (such as the Slater condition).** 
- For non-convex problems, points satisfying the KKT conditions may be local optima, saddle points, or even extreme points.
